# Summary of achievements

Amazon Online Shopping

Amazon online shopping is part of our life now and is important for us to think about how to identify the goods online. Through previous analysis of price of products, it can be concluded that during Covid-19, the price kept the same or increased, but it falls down recently maybe because of the better condition and more people getting vaccinated.

There is high differentiability in the words used throughout these reviews of different rates through clustering analysis.

Through twitter data, the discussion of amazon or online shopping happened between a small number of people.

Association Rule Mining (ARM) is a method of analysis that utilizes relational information to identify items that appear frequently together. Here, this analysis looks specifically at which words frequently appear together across hundreds of reviews. One can clearly see by the relationships described that there were several words that were commonly used for reviews. # What need to improve?

Amazon Online Shopping

It is a pity that part of Amazon data like price of shipping fee or customers age or place, cannot be found online with API or web scraping, so it is hard to figure out the differences. Additionally, some models don't perform well and have a low accuracy.

For example, the fashion reviews may because people are used to use so many words to express and some words are meaningless or used frequently. And the analysis of price changes always have low accuracies may because the parameters that are being used to time, brand, type etc. are not sufficient parameters to distinguish the price change of products. The sales rank of partly products between categories are hardly to distinguish and we used clustering to analysis.

It can be found that cluster k=2 would be the optimal choice meaning that the partial data would just be separated to 2 categories: Women and Men and don't need to differentiate more finely. But this conclusion is limited to the partial data and need to apply to more categories and larger data.
