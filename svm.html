# Conclusion

Linear kernel achieves the best result in classifying "5 stars" comments at 82% accuracy which seems to have the similar results with that in R. Cost parameter does not offer much help to the classifier.

Interestingly, even though the SVM classifier with Linear kernel has strong performance, there doesn't seem a single strong indicative words that can lead to "5 stats" comments. Therefore, the predictive power of the classifier comes from the effect of a combination of words.

"e1071" package is used to construct the naive bayes algorithm with different kernels. Across all four kernels: linear, polynomial, sigmoid, and radial, the missclassification rate was rather high at 50%, except that the linear kernel is just a little better. And "cost" parameter does not offer much help in this case. Additionally, in looking at the four confusion matrices, the false positive rate is rather high. Essentially if the type of price change is not correctly classified as a up, then the model will missclassify it.

# Conclusion

The model showed that based on the data used, it might be hard to classify the trend of price changes used in for products based on time, brand and type. There are a couple of ways to interpret these results. The first is that the parameters that are being used to time, brand, type etc. are not sufficient parameters to distinguish the price change of products.
